## Setting: The Shattered Meridian

### Timeline Context

**The Cataclysm** (100 years before game start): A planetary alignment triggered seismic collapse. The single supercontinent fractured into an archipelago of three major landmasses and dozens of smaller islands. Technological regression followed; societies reorganized around surviving city-states.

**The Long Silence** (60–100 years ago): Diplomatic isolation. Each faction rebuilt independently, developing distinct cultures and ideologies.

**Present Day**: Scouts and traders are rediscovering neighboring cultures. Tensions rise. Resources are unevenly distributed across the new geography.

### The Three Continents

**Meridian Prime** (largest landmass):
- Temperate climate, fertile river valleys
- Rich history; pre-Cataclysm ruins everywhere
- Home to **Harmonists** and **Archivists**

**Isolde** (second landmass):
- Harsh, fractured terrain; deep canyons and plateaus
- Sparse resources; difficult farming
- Home to **Isolationists**

**The Verdant Reaches** (lush archipelago):
- Tropical climate; dangerous megafauna; dense jungles
- Rich biodiversity; unique flora/fauna
- Home to **Adaptors**

## The Four Factions

### 1. Harmonists

**Philosophy:** Cooperation, shared resources, collective prosperity.

**Strengths:**
- Units heal nearby allies (+2 HP per turn if adjacent)
- Shared resource pools between armies
- Bonuses for unit formations (3+ units in 2-square radius = +1 armor all)

**Weaknesses:**
- Individual unit strength is lower than other factions
- Require coordination; playing scattered units is ineffective
- Tech tree emphasizes group abilities over solo heroes

**Campaign Hook:** *The Unification Route* — Harmonists seek to rebuild the pre-Cataclysm alliance and restore peace through diplomacy and integration.

**Core Story:** A visionary leader believes the only path to stability is binding the four factions through mutual prosperity. Mission 1 involves defending refugee settlements; Mission 5 is diplomacy with rival factions.

---

### 2. Isolationists

**Philosophy:** Self-sufficiency, individual strength, independence.

**Strengths:**
- Individual units are significantly stronger than other factions
- Veterans gain permanent stat increases (+1 to chosen stat per level 5)
- Can generate resources even with zero territory (slower, but independent)

**Weaknesses:**
- No healing without dedicated tech tree investment
- No cooperative bonuses; playing units together gives no benefit
- Tech tree emphasizes lone-wolf gameplay

**Campaign Hook:** *The Fortress Route* — Isolationists fortify strongholds and resist external influence. Early missions defend territory; later missions launch precision strikes.

**Core Story:** An isolationist warlord views the rediscovery of other factions as an existential threat. They must secure borders and prove Isolationist self-sufficiency can outlast any alliance.

---

### 3. Adaptors

**Philosophy:** Evolution, flexibility, environmental mastery.

**Strengths:**
- Units can "morph" into different roles (Warrior → Scout → Mage, etc.)
- Morph abilities are free once learned (3-turn cooldown)
- Environmental bonuses in jungle terrain (move faster, heal in forests)

**Weaknesses:**
- Morphing limits specialization (a unit in Scout form loses Warrior armor)
- Resource generation lower than others
- Tech tree costs more; fewer total units available

**Campaign Hook:** *The Adaptation Route* — Adaptors believe survival means evolving. They view the Cataclysm as an opportunity, not a catastrophe. Missions emphasize adapting to environmental challenges.

**Core Story:** An Adaptor diplomat wants to prove morphing can solve the conflicts that other factions cause. Early missions require adapting to harsh terrain; later missions show morphing in diplomacy.

---

### 4. Archivists

**Philosophy:** Knowledge, history, asymmetric advantage through information.

**Strengths:**
- Reveal opponent tech trees and army composition
- Can "copy" learned techs at 80% cost (powerful but limited)
- Units with high experience get special "archivist knowledge" buffs

**Weaknesses:**
- Unit roster is smallest
- Early game is slow (low production)
- Knowledge bonuses require experience investment

**Campaign Hook:** *The Restoration Route* — Archivists rebuild pre-Cataclysm libraries and seek ancient weapons. Missions involve discovering ruins and deciphering tech.

**Core Story:** An Archivist scholar discovers that the Cataclysm wasn't random—it was engineered. Their campaign is about uncovering the truth and preventing a second catastrophe.

## Campaign Structure

### Act I: Awakening (Missions 1–5)

Each faction's introduction. Players learn their faction's playstyle and meet key characters.

- **Mission 1:** Defend home settlement (tutorial + narrative intro)
- **Mission 2:** Capture neighboring territory (first expansion)
- **Mission 3:** Face faction-specific challenge (Harmonists: diplomacy, Isolationists: solo unit test, etc.)
- **Mission 4:** Defend against first "outsider" contact
- **Mission 5:** Major plot twist; invitation to unite against larger threat

### Act II: Conflict (Missions 6–12)

Factions collide. Alliance/conflict choices made at Mission 5 branch the story.

**Harmonist Path:** Negotiation attempts with other factions; missions involve protecting allies.
**Isolationist Path:** Defensive stronghold battles; repelling invasions.
**Adaptor Path:** Environmental survival; learning to morph in combat.
**Archivist Path:** Discovering ruins; unlocking ancient tech.

### Act III: Convergence (Missions 13–18)

All paths merge for a climactic revelation: the Cataclysm wasn't natural. A hidden faction is manipulating events.

**Final Mission:** A race to an ancient artifact that can prevent another Cataclysm or weaponize it.

Victory is faction-specific:
- **Harmonists:** Unite all factions; prevent the Cataclysm
- **Isolationists:** Secure artifact for solo power
- **Adaptors:** Use artifact to reshape the world
- **Archivists:** Unlock the truth behind the Cataclysm

---

> **Narrative Design:** Every mission should reinforce the faction's identity. A Harmonist mission should feel collaborative; an Isolationist mission should feel like a lone stand. Use mission design (map layout, unit types, objectives) to support narrative.
